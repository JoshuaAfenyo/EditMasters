Editing & Proofreading Tips

Beginners

  • Day 1: Learn basic sentence structure and punctuation.
  • Day 2: Practice identifying common grammar mistakes.
  • Day 3: Edit a short paragraph for clarity and tone.
  • Day 4: Explore the use of style guides (APA, MLA, etc.).
  • Day 5: Edit a sample document using all previous tips.

Intermediate

  • Day 1: Review subject-verb agreement in complex sentences.
  • Day 2: Practice editing for tone and voice consistency.
  • Day 3: Identify and correct wordiness or redundancy.
  • Day 4: Understand transitions and improve flow between paragraphs.
  • Day 5: Edit for parallel structure in lists and comparisons.
  • Day 6: Refine punctuation: colons, semicolons, and dashes.
  • Day 7: Learn to spot vague or abstract language and replace it with specificity.
  • Day 8: Ensure consistent verb tenses throughout documents.
  • Day 9: Practice rewriting passive voice into active voice.
  • Day 10: Edit a multi-paragraph sample piece for structure, tone, and clarity.

Focus One Step at a Time

Edit for grammar first, then look at flow, tone, and consistency.

Use Tools—Then Rely on Yourself

Use tools like Grammarly, but always do a human review for context and tone.
